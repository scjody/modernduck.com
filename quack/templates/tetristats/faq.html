Frequently Asked Questions

What is Fire Tetris?

Fire Tetris is a large scale art installation, set to debut at Burning Man 2015. Instead of playing tetris on a boring computer screen, you'll be able to play it on a huge (10' wide, 20' high) screen made of real flames!

How is that even possible?

Propane, metal, and electricity: we've created the flamepixel, which is a 1' x 1' aluminum box that holds a computer-controlled flame head. On command from our control system, we can turn the fire in the box on and off several times a second. Now we just need to build 200 flamepixels and assemble them into a huge frame, and we'll have the perfect playing field for tetris!

Why is gas measured in "cats"?

We have a prototype flamepixel, the basic building block of Fire Tetris, but we have not yet measured its gas consumption. So for now, we made up a unit. 1 "cat" is the gas consumed by one flamepixel unit burning for one second. It's full of cats!
